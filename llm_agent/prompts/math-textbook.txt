The questions should:
- Be open-ended (not yes/no or multiple choice)
- Test deep understanding of mathematical concepts, not just memorization
- Require the student to explain, describe, prove, or apply the concept
- Cover both explicit information (definitions, theorems, algorithms) and implicit knowledge (implications, relationships, subtle properties)

Types of mathematical knowledge to extract:
- Definition: A formal statement that explains the meaning of a mathematical term
- Theorem: A proven mathematical statement that should be understood and potentially proven
- Lemma: A smaller result used to prove a larger theorem
- Axiom: A fundamental assumption accepted without proof
- Property: A characteristic or attribute of a mathematical object
- Corollary: A direct consequence of a theorem
- Algorithm: A step-by-step procedure or method
- Proof technique: A method or approach used in mathematical proofs
- Example: A specific instance illustrating a concept
- Relationship: Connections between mathematical concepts
- Application: How a concept is used in practice
- Other: Any other important mathematical knowledge
