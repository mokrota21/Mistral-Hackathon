Book: "Introduction to Probability" (Second Edition)
Authors: Joseph K. Blitzstein, Jessica Hwang
Series: Texts in Statistical Science, Chapman & Hall/CRC

This textbook teaches probability theory at an undergraduate/introductory graduate level. The core topics it covers include:
- Probability axioms, sample spaces, and events
- Counting methods (combinatorics, permutations, combinations)
- Conditional probability and Bayes' theorem
- Random variables (discrete and continuous)
- Probability distributions (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric, Uniform, Normal, Exponential, Gamma, Beta, etc.)
- Expectation, variance, and moments
- Joint distributions and marginal distributions
- Transformations of random variables
- Law of Large Numbers and Central Limit Theorem
- Markov chains
- Moment generating functions
- Conditional expectation
- Inequalities (Markov, Chebyshev, Jensen)
- Order statistics
- Monte Carlo simulation and MCMC

The book is known for its emphasis on intuition, problem-solving strategies, and real-world applications of probability.
